# Master Sources & Further Reading

Every hard fact, date, name, and number used in the series is listed here, grouped by topic, with a link for verification. Each episode also carries a short "Sources" box citing the items most relevant to it.

**Note on use:** all sources below were *paraphrased*, not quoted at length, in the scripts. When you publish, keep the attributions so viewers can check the claims themselves. Where reputable sources disagree (most importantly on how *effective* Cambridge Analytica really was), the scripts present the disagreement rather than picking a winner.

## Campaign A — Edward Bernays, Lucky Strike & "Torches of Freedom"

- "Torches of Freedom," Wikipedia — overview of the 1929 Easter Sunday Parade stunt. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torches_of_Freedom
- "Public relations campaigns of Edward Bernays," Wikipedia — Bernays' consultation with psychoanalyst A. A. Brill; the "torches of freedom" framing; the green-color campaign. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_relations_campaigns_of_Edward_Bernays
- "Edward Bernays," Wikipedia — biography; "father of public relations"; *Propaganda* (1928) and *Crystallizing Public Opinion* (1923). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Bernays
- American Historical Association, *Perspectives* — the staged Easter parade of debutantes lighting cigarettes "in the interest of the equality of the sexes." https://www.historians.org/perspectives-article/
- The New Yorker, "The Spin Myth" (1998) — the ten chosen women, the detailed instructions on when to light cigarettes; skeptical view of the PR legend. https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/1998/07/06/the-spin-myth
- Truth Initiative, "Three key moments in the history of marketing tobacco to women" — Bernays used the equality angle; recruited women including his own secretary. https://truthinitiative.org/research-resources/tobacco-industry-marketing/three-key-moments-history-marketing-tobacco-women
- Stanford Research Into the Impact of Tobacco Advertising (SRITA), "20,679 Physicians" — the physician-endorsement ads ran 1928–1932 claiming Luckies were "less irritating." https://tobacco.stanford.edu/cigarettes/doctors-smoking/20679-physicians/
- "Lucky Strike," Wikipedia — "Reach for a Lucky instead of a sweet"; sales rose from 14 billion (1925) to 40 billion (1930). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucky_Strike
- "From social taboo to 'torch of freedom': the marketing of cigarettes to women," *Tobacco Control* (BMJ), 2000. https://tobaccocontrol.bmj.com/content/9/1/3
- New York Times obituary of Edward Bernays (1995, via Internet Archive) — making green fashionable after the package color clashed with women's clothing. https://web.archive.org/web/20180124200210/http://www.nytimes.com/books/98/08/16/specials/bernays-obit.html
- W. Lawrence (Larson), "Edward Bernays's 1929 'Torches of Freedom' March" (*Journalism History*, 2014) — argues the march is a textbook pseudo-event and questions parts of the standard legend. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/08821127.2015.1064681

## Campaign B — 2016-era influence operations

### Internet Research Agency (IRA) / "troll farm"
- U.S. House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence — IRA social-media ad archive; notes Special Counsel Mueller indicted 13 Russian individuals and 3 organizations on Feb 16, 2018. https://intelligence.house.gov/social-media-content/
- "Mueller report," Wikipedia — overview of *Report on the Investigation into Russian Interference in the 2016 Presidential Election* (released April 18, 2019). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mueller_report
- Office of Sen. Mark Warner / U.S. Senate Select Committee on Intelligence — bipartisan reporting on Russia's use of social media; flooding platforms with false reports and exploiting existing divisions. https://www.warner.senate.gov/
- NPR, "New Reports Detail Expansive Russia Disinformation Scheme Targeting U.S." (Dec 17, 2018) — the two reports prepared for the Senate (New Knowledge; Oxford Internet Institute/Graphika); extensive targeting of African-Americans. https://www.npr.org/2018/12/17/677390345/
- Privacy International summary of the Senate reports — operations "played both sides," posting racist content to right-leaning audiences and equality content to Black Americans, to widen divisions. https://www.privacyinternational.org/examples/2791/

### Cambridge Analytica
- "Facebook–Cambridge Analytica data scandal," Wikipedia — the quiz app; data of up to 87 million profiles harvested; Christopher Wylie's 2018 disclosures. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facebook%E2%80%93Cambridge_Analytica_data_scandal
- The Guardian, "Cambridge Analytica: how did it turn clicks into votes?" (May 6, 2018) — Wylie explains the modeling pipeline. https://www.theguardian.com/news/2018/may/06/cambridge-analytica-how-turn-clicks-into-votes-christopher-wylie
- BBC News, "Cambridge Analytica academic a 'scapegoat'" (2018) — Aleksandr Kogan; ~50 million figure. https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-43480978

### The important nuance — was it actually effective?
- Fortune, "Cambridge Analytica's Psychographic Models Were Mostly Hype" (2018) — CA reportedly admitted "psychographics" were not used in the Trump campaign. https://fortune.com/2018/03/25/cambridge-analytica-wasnt-quite-what-it-claimed-to-be/
- Nature, "The scant science behind Cambridge Analytica's controversial marketing techniques" (2018). https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-03880-4
- The Guardian, "Cambridge Analytica did not misuse data in EU referendum, says watchdog" (Oct 7, 2020) — UK Information Commissioner Elizabeth Denham's findings. https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2020/oct/07/cambridge-analytica-did-not-misuse-data-in-eu-referendum-says-watchdog
- Jakee & Fink, "Microtargeting Voters in the 2016 US Election: Was Cambridge Analytica Really Different?" (2024) — concludes CA was broadly similar to competitors and likely less effective than the hype. https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4843786

## Frameworks & psychology of persuasion

- Institute for Propaganda Analysis (1937) — the seven classic "propaganda devices": name-calling, glittering generalities, transfer, testimonial, plain folks, card stacking, bandwagon. Overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda_techniques
- Robert Cialdini, *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* — six principles: reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Cialdini
- Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — the "System 1 / System 2" model of fast vs. slow thinking (used as the series' backbone metaphor).

### Specific effects cited by episode
- **Illusory-truth effect** — Hasher, Goldstein & Toppino (1977), "Frequency and the conference of referential validity." Overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illusory_truth_effect
- **Mere-exposure effect** — Zajonc (1968), "Attitudinal effects of mere exposure." Overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mere-exposure_effect
- **Framing effect / prospect theory** — Tversky & Kahneman (1981), "The framing of decisions and the psychology of choice" (the "Asian disease problem"). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7455683/
- **Social identity / in-group bias** — Henri Tajfel et al., minimal-group experiments; social identity theory. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_identity_theory
- **Scapegoating** — overview. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scapegoating
- **Astroturfing** — term attributed to U.S. Sen. Lloyd Bentsen (1985). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astroturfing and https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/astroturfing
- **Pseudo-event** — concept from Daniel J. Boorstin, *The Image* (1962): an event staged mainly in order to be reported.
- **Dog whistle (politics)** — Ian Haney López, *Dog Whistle Politics* (2014). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog_whistle_(politics)
- **Whataboutism** — a form of the *tu quoque* fallacy; associated with Soviet/Russian propaganda. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whataboutism
- **Firehose of falsehood** — Paul & Matthews, RAND Corporation (2016), *The Russian "Firehose of Falsehood" Propaganda Model* (PE-198). https://www.rand.org/pubs/perspectives/PE198.html
- **Glittering generalities / virtue words** — Institute for Propaganda Analysis (see above).

## Module 3 — Coercive interrogation (the Reid technique), lie detection & scams (Episodes 23–30)

### The Reid technique itself
- "Reid technique," Wikipedia — origins with John E. Reid (polygraph examiner, former Chicago officer) and Fred Inbau; high-pressure-then-sympathy structure. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reid_technique
- U.S. Office of Justice Programs (OJP) — abstract listing the nine steps (positive confrontation, theme development, handling denials, overcoming objections, procurement/retention of attention, handling passive mood, alternative question, developing details, written confession). https://www.ojp.gov/ncjrs/virtual-library/abstracts/reid-technique-interviewing-and-interrogation-investigative
- Inbau, Reid, Buckley & Jayne, *Criminal Interrogation and Confessions* — the foundational manual (1st ed. 1962). OJP abstract: https://www.ojp.gov/ncjrs/virtual-library/abstracts/criminal-interrogation-and-confessions-fourth-edition
- John E. Reid & Associates — the owners' position that the method, properly applied, is not coercive; and their newer "Reid PEACE" program. https://reid.com/

### Lie detection (Episode 24)
- **~54% accuracy:** Bond, C. F., & DePaulo, B. M. (2006), "Accuracy of Deception Judgments," *Personality and Social Psychology Review* — meta-analysis of 200+ studies; avg. 54% (≈47% of lies, ≈61% of truths). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16859438/
- **Professionals no better than laypeople:** Bond & DePaulo (2008), "Individual differences in judging deception"; Aamodt & Custer review (professionals ≈ 55%). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18605814/
- **"Lying eyes"/NLP eye-direction myth debunked:** Wiseman, Watt, ten Brinke, Porter, Couper & Rankin (2012), "The Eyes Don't Have It: Lie Detection and Neuro-Linguistic Programming," *PLOS ONE*. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3394779/
- **Polygraph not a valid lie detector:** National Research Council / National Academy of Sciences (2003), *The Polygraph and Lie Detection* — physiological responses not uniquely related to deception; accuracy insufficient for screening. https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/the-polygraph-and-lie-detection

### False confessions, risk factors & reform (Episodes 26, 28, 29)
- **Risk factors & recommendations:** Kassin, S. M., et al. (2010), "Police-Induced Confessions: Risk Factors and Recommendations," *Law and Human Behavior* (AP-LS Scientific Review Paper); updated 2.0 in 2025. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20112057/
- **~25% of DNA exonerations involved false confessions/admissions:** Innocence Project. https://innocenceproject.org/overturning-false-confessions/ and https://innocenceproject.org/research-resources/
- **~30% in an analysis of 194 DNA exonerations:** Garrett, B. (2011) and related analysis. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21721941/
- **Juveniles especially at risk:** Innocence Project, "False Confessions More Prevalent Among Teens." https://innocenceproject.org/news/false-confessions-more-prevalent-among-teens/
- **Major trainer drops Reid (2017):** The Marshall Project, "The Seismic Change in Police Interrogations"; Innocence Project summary. https://www.themarshallproject.org/2017/03/07/the-seismic-change-in-police-interrogations
- **PEACE model (England & Wales, since ~1992):** "PEACE method of interrogation," Wikipedia; aims at a full account, not a confession. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PEACE_method_of_interrogation

### Rights (Episode 29)
- Right to silence and to counsel (U.S.): *Miranda v. Arizona* (1966); on the need to invoke silence clearly, *Berghuis v. Thompkins* (2010). Rights vary by jurisdiction; the episode notes this is education, not legal advice.

### The street playbook — scams & cults (Episode 30)
- **Foot-in-the-door:** Freedman, J. L., & Fraser, S. C. (1966), "Compliance without pressure: The foot-in-the-door technique," *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*. Overview: https://www.simplypsychology.org/compliance.html
- **Love bombing & high-control groups (BITE model — Behavior, Information, Thought, Emotional control):** Steven Hassan, *Combating Cult Mind Control*; https://freedomofmind.com/cult-mind-control/bite-model-pdf-download/
- **Consumer anti-fraud guidance** (urgency, isolation, "hang up and call back" verification): e.g., U.S. Federal Trade Commission consumer advice. https://consumer.ftc.gov/ (verify current local resources before publishing)
