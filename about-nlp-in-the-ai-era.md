## Two very different things share one acronym

"NLP" in the AI era is confusing because it refers to two unrelated things:

1. **Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)** — the 1970s self-help / "covert persuasion" system. As covered in Episode 62, decades of independent research found **no credible evidence** for its core claims; reviewers have called it pseudoscientific. *This is the snake oil.*
2. **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** — the legitimate field of computer science behind chatbots, translation, and large language models (LLMs). *This is real technology.*

They are not the same. One is a debunked persuasion myth; the other is the engineering that powers modern AI.

## Why the AI era matters for both

**The pseudoscience gets a new coat of paint.** Expect "AI-powered covert influence," "neuro-AI persuasion," and "reprogram your subconscious with AI" courses. It's the same Episode-62 snake oil, now wrapped in "AI" buzzwords to borrow fresh authority. The red flags are unchanged: sciencey jargon, a guru, a proprietary "secret" method, testimonials instead of evidence, and a price tag.

**The real risk is the genuine technology.** The honest danger isn't that Neuro-Linguistic Programming finally works — it's that *real* AI lets ordinary manipulation scale and personalize in ways this series warns about:

- **Hyper-personalized persuasion** at scale (Episode 13 — microtargeting), now cheap and automatic.
- **Synthetic media** — fake voices, faces, and video (Episode 36 — deepfakes).
- **Bots that pass as people** (Episode 14 — sockpuppets), now far more convincing.
- **An infinite firehose** of tailored, contradictory content (Episode 22).

So hold the same two-sided skepticism Episode 62 teaches: be unimpressed by "AI mind-power" snake oil, **and** take the real, evidence-based risks of AI-enabled manipulation seriously.

## The defense is unchanged

The master toolkit from the series finale (Episode 72) works here too: ask *who's talking and who pays*, *name the feeling*, *pause*, *separate the lever from the thing*, *find what's missing*, *follow the money*, *keep your anchors*, and *don't trade naivety for cynicism*. New technology, same calm question: **what's really happening here?**
